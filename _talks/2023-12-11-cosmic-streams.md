Electromagnetic follow-up of gravitational wave events remains a significant focus of astronomical research due to the increased precision and range of measurements possible with multi-messenger data.
A critical part of follow-up efforts is the rapid initial discovery of electromagnetic counterparts, which can be challenged by large event localizations and is necessary for more precise follow-up with technology such as JWST.
I discuss the follow-up strategy of out group with DECam for O4, and present accomplishments.
In particular I outline our image processing pipeline that enables near real-time automated detection of astrophysical transients, and its interface with existing tools such as SkyPortal for streamlined communication of our findings.
